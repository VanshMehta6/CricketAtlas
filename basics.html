Cricket Basics

What is Cricket?

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of 11 players on a circular field with a 22-yard pitch in the center. Originating in England in the 16th century, it is now the second-most popular sport globally with over 2.5 billion fans.

Key Rules & Gameplay

  • Overs: Each over consists of 6 legal deliveries. Limited-overs cricket has a set number of overs per innings (20, 50, etc.).
  • Ways to Score: Running between wickets (1-3 runs), boundaries (4 runs if ball touches ground, 6 if cleared without bouncing), extras (no balls, wides, byes, leg byes).
  • Ways to Get Out: Bowled, caught, LBW (leg before wicket), run out, stumped, hit wicket, handled the ball (rare), obstructing the field, timed out.
  • Umpires: Two on-field umpires make decisions, with a third umpire (TV umpire) for close calls and reviews.
  • DRS: Decision Review System allows each team to challenge umpire decisions, using ball-tracking and ultra-edge technology.

Cricket international Explained

  • Test Cricket: The oldest format, played over 5 days with unlimited overs. Teams bat twice each.
  • ODI (One Day International): 50 overs per side. World Cup held every 4 years since 1975.
  • T20I (Twenty20 International): 20 overs per side. Fastest and most explosive format, introduced in 2005.

Famous Cricket Grounds

  • Lord's Cricket Ground, London — "The Home of Cricket" since 1814
  • Melbourne Cricket Ground (MCG) — Capacity 100,000, hosted 1992 World Cup final
  • Eden Gardens, Kolkata — India's oldest and largest stadium
  • Sydney Cricket Ground (SCG) — Known for its iconic Members Pavilion
  • Narendra Modi Stadium, Ahmedabad — World's largest cricket stadium (132,000 capacity)
