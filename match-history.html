Iconic Matches in Cricket History

Date: July 14, 2019 | Venue: Lord's, London

The most dramatic final in cricket history. Scores were tied after 50 overs (241 each) and again in the Super Over (15 each). England won on the boundary countback rule (26-17). Ben Stokes was awarded Player of the Match for his heroic 84* [international:1].

Date: March 11-15, 2001 | Venue: Eden Gardens, Kolkata

India became only the third team in Test history to win after following on. VVS Laxman scored 281 and Harbhajan Singh took a hat-trick. India won by 171 runs after Australia had enforced the follow-on.

Date: June 29, 2024 | Venue: Kensington Oval, Barbados

India defended 176/7 against South Africa, winning by 7 runs. Virat Kohli scored 76 off 49, while Hardik Pandya took 3/20. Jasprit Bumrah was named Player of the domestic [international:2].

Date: April 3, 2016 | Venue: Eden Gardens, Kolkata

Carlos Brathwaite hit four consecutive sixes off Ben Stokes in the final over to seal a dramatic win. West Indies chased 156 with 4 wickets in hand [international:2].

Date: November 19, 2023 | Venue: Narendra Modi Stadium, Ahmedabad

Australia won their 6th ODI World Cup, defeating India by 6 wickets. Travis Head scored 137 off 120 balls and was named Player of the Match [international:1].

Date: August 7-11, 2005 | Venue: Edgbaston, Birmingham

The closest Ashes finish in history. England won by 2 runs after Australia needed 3 runs with 1 wicket left. Andrew Flintoff consoled Brett Lee in a moment of sportsmanship that defined the series.

Date: June 25, 1983 | Venue: Lord's, London

India defeated the two-time defending champions West Indies by 43 runs. Kapil Dev's iconic catch of Viv Richards shifted momentum. India's victory sparked a cricketing revolution in the country [international:1].

Date: March 17, 1996 | Venue: Gaddafi Stadium, Lahore

Sri Lanka won their first World Cup, defeating Australia by 7 wickets. Aravinda de Silva scored 107* and took 3/42, earning Player of the Match [international:1].

Date: June 11-15, 2025 | Venue: Lord's, London

South Africa won their first ICC title since 1998, chasing 236 runs with 5 wickets in hand. Aiden Markram scored 136 in the fourth innings and was named Player of the Match [international:3].

Date: September 24, 2007 | Venue: Wanderers, Johannesburg

The inaugural T20 World Cup final. India defended 157/5, winning by 5 runs. Irfan Pathan took 3/16 and was Player of the Match [international:2].
